\begin{center}
\Large\bf
	Response to the Referee's Comment to DE10856 
\end{center}

	To a great extent we agree with the referee that the new spectrum that has been proposed
	needs a thorough semi-classical check.
	We advocate however, that a complete semi-classical analysis is a full-fledged task, 
	which we plan to address in a separate survey. 
	Besides the obvious task of finding and properly interpreting the quantum corrections to the masses of the solitons, 
	such a survey also includes a study and classification of the similar states in the four dimensional
	gauge theory, where we believe the same spectrum holds at the root of the baryonic Higgs branch.
	We agree that the new states closely hint to the bound states of monopoles and quarks. 
	Such states in the CP($N-1$) theory were discussed in \cite{Dorey:1999zk}.
	Whether our states are indeed such or not, is an important question and part of this problem as well.
	Regardless of this, implications for four dimensions, if any, inferred from the two-dimensional mass spectrum 
	must have fundamental significance for the gauge theory.

	We have devoted a brief overview section ``Prospects for the Spectrum in Quasi-Classics'' 
	in our paper to the key feature of the spectrum.
	Namely, the new towers of states differ from each other (as well as from the previously known one \cite{Dor})
	by a fractional U(1) charge contribution, which occurs due to the presence of fermions. 
	We argue that such a contribution is not new to this theory, and is known for CP(1) \cite{ls1,Shifman:2005rs}.
	Although in the latter theory this does not lead to the emergence of new states, it renders it very plausible that 
	the same effect is responsible for the mass splitting in CP($N-1$).
	Overall, we believe that the semi-classical derivation of the new spectrum is a multi-face problem, 
	and the right topic for discussion in a separate work.
